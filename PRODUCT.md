# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students preparing for the UCAT (UK medical/dental school admissions test), typically under real time pressure and exam anxiety. They open the app to do one of a small number of concrete things: practice questions, sit a timed mock, review flashcards, or check where they stand. They are not browsing — they arrived with a task.

## Product Purpose

UCATify is a UCAT exam-prep tool: practice question bank, timed mock exams, spaced-repetition flashcards, a study scheduler, and an AI tutor, all tracking a student's own analytics over time. Success looks like a student who opens the app and is doing something useful (usually practicing) within seconds, with zero doubt about what to click next.

## Brand Personality

Confident, credible, uncluttered. The tone of a tool that respects the user's time and doesn't need to perform enthusiasm to be trusted — competence shown through restraint, not decoration. Motion should read as precise and intentional (an interface that responds correctly), not playful or gamified.

## Anti-references

- Generic templated SaaS: purple-to-blue gradients, identical icon+heading+text card grids, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section — the "AI made this" tells.
- Busy/gamified edtech: no cartoon mascots, badge-collecting, or loud gamification. This is a serious tool for a high-stakes exam, not a children's app.
- Cluttered dashboard-with-everything: avoid dense, everything-visible-at-once layouts. One clear next action beats ten equally-weighted options.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice is the front door.** Every other feature (Guide, Scheduler, Tutor, Leaderboard) supports practicing; none should compete with it for the first click.
2. **One primary action per screen.** When several things are technically possible, one is visually obvious.
3. **Show, don't perform.** Confidence comes from correctness and restraint (real data, precise motion, honest empty states) not from decorative flourishes.
4. **Motion confirms, it doesn't entertain.** Animate state changes and feedback; never animate for its own sake or at the cost of perceived speed.
5. **Respect exam-day stakes.** Copy and pacing should feel calm and competent — this is closer to a cockpit instrument than a consumer app.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 contrast for body text, ≥3:1 for large/bold text, visible keyboard focus states, and full `prefers-reduced-motion` support for every animation introduced.
